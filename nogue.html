ABOUT THE PROJECT

NOGUE is a satirical fashion magazine that appropriates the visual codes of luxury editorials to critique the system it imitates. Conceived as a biannual publication, it questions consumer culture, fast fashion, and performative luxury through parody, irony, and exaggerated aesthetics. Referencing publications such as Vogue, The Gentlewoman, and Toiletpaper, NOGUE borrows the authority and polish of high-end editorial design only to distort it. Bold typography, vibrant colors, theatrical photography, and hand-drawn interventions disrupt traditional layouts, creating tension between gloss and critique.

The result is a publication that exists between collectible magazine and rebellious zine — a space where fashion is both celebrated and dismantled through humor, visual impact, and cultural commentary.
